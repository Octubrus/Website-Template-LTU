# COPY_RULES.md

Rules for writing copy on demo websites built from this starter.

## Language

- The default language is **Lithuanian**.
- Write naturally. Avoid stiff translations from English templates.
- Avoid forced em-dash style and hype words ("modernūs sprendimai", "geriausi rinkoje", "Nr. 1", "unikalu") unless the claim is concrete and provable.
- Avoid corporate filler.

## Placeholder copy

- **Do not use lorem ipsum** unless absolutely necessary to test spacing.
- Use **purposeful instructional placeholder copy** — copy that tells the future editor what should go there.

Good placeholder examples:
- "Trumpas sakinys apie pagrindinę paslaugą, vietą ir kodėl klientui verta susisiekti."
- "Čia įrašykite 1–2 sakinius apie tai, kam ši paslauga skirta ir kokią naudą gauna klientas."
- "Detalus paslaugos aprašymas. Kas įeina, kiek laiko užtrunka, koks rezultatas."

Bad placeholder examples:
- "Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet"
- "Geriausia paslauga mieste!" (fake claim)
- "Tūkstančiai patenkintų klientų" (fake stat)

## Things never to fabricate

- Testimonials and quotes
- Customer names, photos, or logos
- Statistics, ratings, review counts
- Awards, certifications, accreditations
- Team members or credentials
- "Years of experience" numbers
- Geographic claims ("in 10+ cities")

If the client has weak material, **leave a clear placeholder** rather than invent something.

## Section discipline

- **Eyebrow labels**: only when the eyebrow adds real information (location, category). Do not add decorative eyebrows just to fill space.
- **Numbered steps (01 / 02 / 03)**: only when the section is **genuinely** a step-by-step process. Do not number arbitrary feature cards.
- **Stat cards / counters**: only with real numbers the client has approved. Do not add them for visual rhythm.
- **CTAs**: one main CTA across the site. Do not invent five different CTA labels. The secondary action is usually the phone number, email, or contact page link.

## Hero rules

- Hero headline should **state a benefit**, not a feature.
- Hero subheadline = 1–2 sentences explaining who the service is for and why.
- One primary CTA + one optional phone link is enough.
- No multiple competing buttons in the hero.

## SEO copy

- Title: include business name + city when relevant. Keep under ~60 chars.
- Description: 150–160 characters, factual, written for a human, not stuffed with keywords. Mention the city if local search matters.

## Local-business buyer psychology

Local prospects buy on:
1. **Trust** — does this business look real and competent?
2. **Clarity** — what exactly do they offer and what does it cost?
3. **Location** — are they actually in my area?
4. **Easy contact** — can I call or message in two clicks?
5. **Proof** — do real photos, hours, address, and contact details exist?
6. **Low friction** — is there any reason this might be a hassle?

Every section should serve at least one of these. If a section serves none, delete it.
